Break The Barrier

Students or educators looking for free resources are in the right place! There is no denying that the system is unequal.


Luckily there are plenty of resources to get a good score without spending a penny.

How to Master the SAT, for Free

Take a Practice Test

~3 hrs

It's hard to know where to start if you don't know where you are in the first place. Thankfully, College Board (the creator of the test) offers multiple free practice tests to estimate your score!


Once you get your score we can start suggesting some resources and make a plan!

For Any Score

Khan Academy

~? hrs

Khan is the biggest SAT prep resource, with extensive curriculum made in collaboration with College Board themselves! Khan has sections for all areas of the test so you can focus on your weaknesses.


If you log in, Khan Academy will help keep track of your progress so you know what you need to focus on. This resource is amazing for any part of your journey!

College Board Question Bank

~? hrs

Coming from the creators of the tests, these questions are extremely accurate to the test!


There are many questions of all levels, and College Board allows you to sort by topic and difficulty so you can nail the topics that get you down! This resource is your best option for improving already great scores and concentrating on those tricky questions!

Extra: Improving Math Scores

Desmos

? hrs

Recently the SAT went digital, and with that came a new resource: Desmos. Desmos is an online graphing calculator that can actually solve a good portion of the math questions for you if you know how to use it right. (Many students attest to this)


While it is a tool rather than a preparation resource, we recommend becoming familiar with it. You can look up your own resources, or we have a free YouTube channel that explains things pretty well.
